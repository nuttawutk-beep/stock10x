Core Narrative

The AI compute market is following the same trajectory as cloud infrastructure — a once-in-a-generation buildout where the dominant infrastructure provider captures a disproportionate share of value. AWS took ~30% margin of every cloud dollar spent; NVIDIA is taking ~60% gross margin on every AI training dollar.

Why 10x is achievable: If AI CapEx grows from $200B today to $600B+ by 2028, and NVDA sustains 65% market share at 75% gross margins, FY27 EPS could reach $55–65. At a 25–30x P/E (below historical multiple for hyper-growth), the stock would trade at $1,375–$1,950 — representing a 57–123% return from today. The 10x case requires AI becoming non-optional infrastructure across every Fortune 500, driving EPS above $80 by 2029.

Business Model
Hardware Revenue

GPU chips (H100, H200, Blackwell B200) + NVLink switching + DGX SuperPOD systems. ~75% gross margin. Constrained by TSMC CoWoS packaging capacity.

Software Layer (NIM)

NVIDIA NIM microservices — containerised AI models for inference. Software-as-a-service on top of hardware. Higher margin, recurring. Early-stage but growing.

Platform (CUDA + DGX Cloud)

DGX Cloud lets enterprises rent H100 clusters by the hour. CUDA remains the development standard for 90%+ of AI models in production.

Key Risks

Supply chain dependency: 100% reliant on TSMC 4nm/3nm. Any Taiwan geopolitical event is an existential supply risk.

Customer internalization: Google (TPU), Amazon (Trainium), Microsoft (Maia) are building custom chips to reduce NVDA dependency.

Valuation premium: Trading at 35x forward P/E — expensive if AI CapEx cycle moderates or EPS misses.

China export restrictions: US BIS regulations limit H100/A800 exports. China was ~20–25% of data center revenue in FY23.
